Hinatuan Enchanted River

The Hinatuan Enchanted River is a saltwater river created by an underground cave system that filters the water free from dirt or silt which juts out into the stream and flows to the Pacific Ocean, the crystal clear waters of The Hinatuan River creates a beautiful and picturesque site in Mindanao being steeped with its lore and history with the local people of Hinatuan, it is said that the rivers caretakers rings a bell at every noon, signalling the swimmers to vacate the river. The caretaker will then sing the "Hymn of Hinatuan" and will then feed the schools of fish that appear during the hymn, during this feeding time no one is allowed to step into or disturb the river. After feeding time the caretaker leaves and the swimmers are permitted to enter the river.

How to get there

The Hinatuan Enchanted River is located in the Barangay Talisay, Hinatuan Municipality, Surigao del Sur, Caraga Region.

From Manila to Cebu:
1. Take a flight bound for Bancasi Airport, Butuan.
2. Head on to the parking lot and take a bus or van going to Mangagoy, Bislig.
3. Ask to be dropped off at Hinatuan Terminal. The trip will take more than 5 hours.
4. Upon arriving in Hinatuan, charter a habal-habal ride going to the enchanted river.

From Davao: 1. Go to Ecoland Terminal.
2. Get on a bus bound for Mangagoy, Bislig. Travel time is around 5 hours.
3. Once at Bislig, hop on to another bus bound for Hinatuan.
4. Upon arriving in Hinatuan town, charter a habal-habal ride going to the enchanted river.

What to Expect

Because this is a popular site, local tourists flock to the river which can become crowded during the weekends. It is recommended to visit during the weekdays where it is less crowded. At noon the local caretaker of the river will ring its bell to commence its feeding hour, it is advised to respect this tradition by vacating the river until the ritual is completed.
